Visual stimulation at 40Hz—a light or screen flicker occurring 40 times per second—is at the forefront of non-invasive neurological research. While the flicker is fast enough to be subtle to the human eye, the brain perceives it as a distinct rhythmic signal. This signal is believed to trigger a profound biological response in the brain’s microglia, which are essentially the 'custodians' or immune cells of the central nervous system.

In a healthy brain, microglia clear out cellular debris. In brains affected by cognitive decline, these cells often become dormant or ineffective. Research has shown that 40Hz visual entrainment can 'wake up' these microglia, prompting them to consume beta-amyloid and tau proteins—the toxic buildups that interfere with synapse communication. By integrating this flicker into the background of digital games, users receive a passive form of 'light therapy' that works alongside active cognitive challenges. This dual-action approach—clearing the 'pathways' while simultaneously 'driving' through them with puzzles—maximizes the potential for neuroplasticity.

**Key Benefits:**
* **Cellular Debris Clearance:** Activation of microglia to reduce toxic protein buildup.
* **Increased Connectivity:** Strengthens communication between the visual cortex and the hippocampus (the memory center).
* **Circadian Support:** Rhythmic light exposure can assist in maintaining healthy sleep patterns in seniors.
